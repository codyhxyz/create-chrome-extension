# Frisbie — Substack teaser pointing to a LayerX webinar (signal lives at the webinar recording)

## TL;DR

Second announcement post in Frisbie's malicious-extensions series. Part 2 is a **webinar hosted with LayerX** on the risks of browser extensions in corporate environments — credential theft, account takeover, data theft, corporate-credential exposure leading to organization-wide breaches. The substantive content is the webinar recording at `layerx.easywebinar.live`; the Substack page contains one paragraph of framing and the registration link.

## Signal

On-page claims worth noting: extensions are "routinely granted extensive permissions" including access to browsing info, passwords, cookies; enterprise exposure compounds individual risk because corporate credentials are in scope; security professionals "are not sufficiently aware of this danger" and lack skills/tools for risk evaluation. Frisbie frames his contribution as "the risks of browser extensions in corporate environments and what you can do to minimize your risk while still using legitimate extensions."

This is useful framing context — especially the "permission-scope as the primary risk vector" claim, which aligns with the Coditude Purple Potassium extraction — but the on-page text is too thin to extract much more. The webinar itself is where the substance lives.

## Key quotes

> "Browser extensions are routinely granted extensive permissions, allowing access to users' browsing information, passwords, cookies, and more, exposing users to risks such as credential theft, account takeover, and data theft."
> — Matt Frisbie, 2026-04-17 (post date per capture)

> "Many security professionals are not sufficiently aware of this danger and don't have the appropriate skills and tools to evaluate their risk and take proper precautions to mitigate it."
> — Matt Frisbie, 2026-04-17
